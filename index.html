My research focuses on developing "curious" AI agents that can autonomously discover and learn a diversity of structures and skills in their environments. In particular, I am interested in transposing these computational models into AI-driven "discovery assistants" that could assist scientists in mapping and navigating the space of possible outcomes of complex self-organizing systems, and in accelerating scientific discovery in these systems.

I recently defended my Ph.D. in Machine Learning under the supervision of Pierre-Yves Oudeyer and Clément Moulin-Frier at the FLOWERS team at Inria, Bordeaux; and in collaboration with the Poietis biotech company. Last year, I also went on a 4-month visit to Dr. Michael Levin and his team at the Levin Lab at Tufts University.

Previously, I have been at University College of London where I completed my Master of Science (MSc) in computer vision and at Télécom Paristech where I did my Master of Engineering (MEng). I also spent a year as an AI research intern at Siemens Healthineers, Princeton N.J., working on deep learning and reinforcement learning algorithms for healthcare.
